Freshness

Four semantic colors signal how a pantry item is doing. Three are time-based — fresh, expiringSoon, expired — and read like a traffic light. The fourth, lowStock, is a quantity signal in warm brown, deliberately off the green-amber-red axis. An unknown item falls back to textTertiary with an empty label.

Freshness hues are reused unchanged in dark mode, mirroring the current code. lowStock brown sits darkest against the raised surface.

Semantics

fresh#43A047 · time · plenty of time left
expiringSoon#FFB300 · time · 1–3 days left
expired#C62828 · time · past expiry / 0 days
lowStock#6D4C41 · quantity · running low
unknowntextTertiary #8B9183 · empty label

lowStock is a different axis. Fresh, expiringSoon, and expired answer “how much time is left?” and run green → amber → red. lowStock answers “how much is left?” — a quantity signal, so it lives off that axis in warm brown to avoid reading as a third point on the time scale. An item can be both fresh and lowStock (e.g. one fresh egg). The applied dot uses a color@8% fill with color@85% bodySmall text.
