Elevation

Shadows are soft, warm, and low. Most surfaces are defined by a 1px border rather than a shadow — the default card is flat. Elevation is reserved for surfaces that float above the page: the FAB, bottom sheets, and snackbars. Only two shadow tokens exist; dark mode deepens both.

Light

flat — default card

Pantry tiles, list rows, ingredient cards. Border-defined, no shadow.

1px border · box-shadow: none

elevation1

FAB at rest, raised panels. The lightest lift off the linen base.

0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,.04)

elevation2

Bottom sheets, snackbars, the FAB while pressed. Deeper, still soft.

0 4px 16px rgba(0,0,0,.07)

Dark

flat — default card

Pantry tiles, list rows, ingredient cards. Border-defined, no shadow.

1px border · box-shadow: none

elevation1

FAB at rest, raised panels. On dark surfaces the shadow runs deeper.

0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,.25)

elevation2

Bottom sheets, snackbars, the FAB while pressed. Deepest of the two.

0 4px 16px rgba(0,0,0,.35)

Dark shadows are intentionally heavier so a lifted surface still separates from the near-black base.

Philosophy & where it's used

  • Border before shadow. Most surfaces — cards, tiles, list rows — read as flat with a 1px border. Shadow is the exception, not the baseline.
  • Low and warm. Both shadows are short-offset and low-opacity, so a lifted surface whispers rather than shouts.
  • Reserved for floating UI. Elevation is applied only to the FAB, bottom sheets, and snackbars — surfaces that genuinely sit above the page.
  • Two steps, no more. The system stops at elevation1 and elevation2; there is no z-axis ladder of five shadows.
